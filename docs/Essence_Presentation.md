# Essence — Project Presentation

Essence grew out of Archimage. Archimage is a Telegram-based game with a magic system: five schools, tiered spells, school bonuses. A working, proven system. At some point it became clear that this system deserves more than what it currently has.

The core idea of Essence — you do not play a character. You play an Essence. A bodiless Sphere that possesses creatures. Every body is temporary. A wolf, a goblin, a mage, a warrior — these are not classes you pick once at the start. They are tools. Possess one, learn its skills, move on. Kill an enemy — take their body mid-combat. Your body dies — it is not game over, it is a reason to find a new one.

There is no analogue to this mechanic. In every MMORPG you are bound to one character. Here, attachment to a body is a tactical decision. A wolf can pass where a human cannot. A mage casts elemental spells. A warrior holds the line. You are one player — but dozens of possible bodies.

The combat system has ten types of physical weapons. The sword slows, the dagger poisons, the hammer crushes armor, the spear knocks back. Each weapon has five unique abilities. On top of that — five schools of elemental magic from Archimage: fire, water, earth, wind, nature. Spells from tier one to tier five. Weapon switching mid-combat with a single key — start the fight with a sword, finish with a bow.

The lore is built on two worlds. A steampunk city where Tesla and Curie discovered the Void — an entity that devours reality. The only way to fight it is to transfer consciousness into a fantasy world, collect the Seal of Elements from four Guardians, and close the warp rifts. The player is that transferred mind. Chapter one — the village of Ashworth, four elemental zones, four bosses. The narrative runs on an "I forgot" mechanic — the body knows the locals, but you do not. The blacksmith is waiting for a stone you supposedly promised to bring. A girl asks for a lotus for her sick father. And you cannot say you do not remember — because the body remembers.

Technically — Phaser 3, TypeScript, browser-based. Procedural generation of textures and sounds. Crafting, a Diablo-style inventory, a quest system, localization in two languages. Everything runs in the browser with no installation.

The project is raw, but the core is in place. The combat system is playable. Bodies switch. Magic from Archimage works in a new context. Next steps — PvP, chapter two, five new schools of magic.
