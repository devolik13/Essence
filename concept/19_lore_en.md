# 19. Lore — World History

---

## Two Worlds

**The Steam World (the player's home)**
Late 19th — early 20th century. The age of steam, electricity, and science.
People believe in progress. But something is wrong — epidemics without cause,
wars that ignite as if on their own, entire cities falling into apathy and fading.
This is not disease. This is not politics. This is the consequence of **the Void**.

**The Fantasy World**
A living world of races, kingdoms, magic, and war. Humans, orcs, elves, undead.
This world knows nothing of the Steam World. Nothing of the Void.
It simply lives by its own rules. Magic here is ordinary — no one questions
where it comes from or how it works.

---

## The Void

The Void is a force without shape and without purpose except one: **to consume**.
It does not destroy — it erases. Creatures, cities, memories.
What the Void absorbs disappears as if it never existed.

The Void is passive — it does not attack on its own.
It simply expands. Slowly. Unstoppably.

But it **generates creatures**.

---

## Semi-Material Beings

The Void generates creatures that exist **between worlds** —
partially in physical reality, partially outside it.
They are not fully material.

This is why technology is useless against them:
- Bullets pass through — not enough dense matter to stop them
- Electric discharge disperses — no conductivity
- Shockwaves cause no harm — pressure doesn't affect the non-material part

They are active. They hunt. They break into the Steam World and consume people —
not killing them, but **erasing** them, leaving empty shells behind.

---

## The Discovery of Tesla and Curie

Nikola Tesla builds an unconventional resonator to measure anomalies
in zones where the creatures appear. Standard instruments show nothing.
Tesla finds something — a frequency that should not exist.
Not electromagnetic. Not acoustic. Something fundamentally different.
He calls it *"the null frequency"*.

Marie Curie creates a counter-wave. At the point of the experiment,
reality begins to behave strangely — as if two spaces briefly touched.
In her measurements she finds **identical oscillations** — but coming
not from the creatures, but from empty air. That was the border
of the Fantasy World.

Their joint conclusion:

> *The Fantasy World vibrates at the same frequency as the Void's creatures.
> The magic of this world is not supernatural. It is physical interaction
> at the right frequency. That is why it works where technology fails.*

This is not magic. This is **resonance**.

---

## The Transfer Machine

Based on this discovery, Tesla and Curie built the **Transfer Machine** —
a device that separates consciousness from the body and sends it
into the Fantasy World in the form of a Sphere.

The body remains in the chamber, connected to the machine.
The consciousness goes there. The Sphere has no physical form —
it can inhabit the bodies of creatures in the Fantasy World,
learn through them, absorb skills and magic.

Every skill, every spell the Sphere learns — is mastery of the right frequencies.
Upon returning home, the Sphere can apply them against the Void's creatures.

The first volunteer — a young assistant from the scientists' circle —
returned. And in battle against the Void's creatures in the real world
he discovered: the skills and magic from the Fantasy World work against them.
The resonance is bidirectional.

From that moment the program received its name — **Essence**.
More machines are built. More Spheres are sent.

## Two Perspectives

A local mage from the Fantasy World and a Sphere look at the same thing
— and see it differently:

| Mage | Sphere |
|---|---|
| "I cast a fire spell" | "You generate oscillations at frequency X" |
| "Magic is a gift from the gods" | "Magic is physics of a different order" |
| "Spirits sometimes inhabit bodies" | "I transferred my consciousness through the Machine" |

Neither is wrong. Just different languages for the same phenomenon.

---

## Void Servants in the Fantasy World

*(Appear in Chapter 2 — absent in Chapter 1)*

The Void did not find the portal immediately.
While Spheres learn in the Fantasy World — it lives by its own rules, untouched.
But with each transfer cycle the portal signal grows stronger.
The Void hears it.

By Chapter 2 its creatures have already entered the Fantasy World.
Here they behave differently than in the Steam World —
they don't grab and erase, they **inhabit the bodies** of living creatures,
using them as shells. Outwardly almost indistinguishable from the living:
same faces, same speech. But their eyes are empty.
Wounds don't bleed. They feel no pain.

Local inhabitants don't understand the nature of the threat.
They call them different things: the cursed, the marked, the unliving.

Spheres know the truth — they've seen this in the Steam World.

## Story Skeleton

**Prologue — The First**
Steam World. Warp rifts appear across Earth — creatures grab people and vanish.
Tesla and Curie discover the null frequency. They build the Transfer Machine.
The first volunteer — a young assistant — goes to the Fantasy World, learns there,
returns. And proves: skills from another world work against the Void.
The Essence program is launched.
Full narrative: [23_prologue_en.md](23_prologue_en.md)

**Chapter 1 — Awakening**
Fantasy World. Everything is unfamiliar. The Sphere inhabits the body of a traveler
already in the village. No one knows about Spheres or the Steam World.
Four zones around the village of Ashworth, four Elemental Guardians.
Defeat each one — prove your strength and receive their magic.
The Void is not here yet — just a living world that lives by its own rules.
Finale: celebration in the village and a troubled messenger from the neighboring
city. Something is happening. Unclear what. For now.

**Chapter 2 — The Shadow**
The Void has found the portal. Its creatures begin entering the Fantasy World
deliberately — inhabiting bodies of the living, acting with coordination.
The Sphere follows the trail, understands the threat is already here.
Bosses: commanders of the Void's servants.

**Chapter 3 — The Node**
The main source of the Void in this world has been found — a place where
the border between worlds has thinned to its limit.
If it's not closed — both worlds fall.
Boss: Voice of the Void — a fragment of the Void itself that has taken form.

**Chapter 4 — Return**
The Node is closed. The way home has opened.
But the Steam World has changed — the creatures are already there,
in the laboratory, on the streets. Final battle in the Steam World
with everything learned in the Fantasy World.
Boss: The Void Unmasked.

## Threat Escalation — The Portal as Point of No Return

By opening the portal between worlds, Tesla and Curie gave Spheres
a path into the Fantasy World. But the Void discovered this passage.

**Chapter 1** — The Void doesn't know about the portal.
Individual creatures in the Fantasy World — chance, not system.
The world is almost untouched.

**Chapter 2** — The Void has found the portal. It begins entering the Fantasy World
deliberately. More servants, organized. Locals feel something has changed
— but don't know why now.

**Chapter 3** — The Void builds a Node in the Fantasy World.
A bridgehead to seize both worlds simultaneously.

**Chapter 4** — Return to the Steam World. Things are worse than before.

---

## Moral Weight

The player bears responsibility for two worlds.

Opening the portal saved the Steam World — gave people a way to learn and fight.
But it also brought the Void into a Fantasy World that had lived peacefully
for thousands of years.

No malice. Just the price of a decision.
And the player lives with that.
