# Humanoid Quests — Chapter 1

Same mechanic as animal quests — the quest appears on possession.
The difference: humanoids have language, relationships, culture.
The Sphere knows them vaguely. Memory is fuzzy. But the body knows what it's supposed to do.

---

## Regular Creatures

---

### Goblin `goblin`
**Weapon:** Dagger | **Skill:** 🗡 Sting — melee, 20% poison

#### Quest: "Night Run"

*[Text on possession]*
> *A small body. Fast. Hungry.*
> *The pack waits at the forest edge. Need to bring something.*
> *Bert's hunting camp is close. Food there. Supplies.*
> *One guard. Half asleep.*

**Objective:** Sneak into Bert's hunting camp, steal supplies, and leave undetected. If the guard wakes — Sting solves the problem quickly and quietly (poison causes sleep, not death).

**Mechanic:** Introduction to stealth and situational poison use. Sting here is not a combat move — it's a quiet neutralization tool.

**Ending:** The pack got food. The goblins melt into the dark.
*50 XP earned. Sting unlocked.*

**World connection:** Bert the next day: *"Someone got into the camp. Clever. Almost no tracks."* If the player went undetected — Bert in Quest 3 says: *"You're careful. That's good for the mine."*

### Orc `orc`
**Weapon:** Greatsword | **Skill:** 🗡 Slashing Strike — melee, 100% bleeding

#### Quest: "The Right to Bear a Blade"

*[Text on possession]*
> *A large body. Heavy. Strong.*
> *The chieftain watches. Two others stand nearby.*
> *The greatsword is driven into the ground, blade down.*
> *Only those who prove their right may take it.*

**Objective:** Defeat two orc challengers in ritual combat. No weapon for the first bout. With weapon for the second. A veteran steps forward for the third — stronger. Slashing Strike is the only way to break his guard and make him bleed.

**Mechanic:** Introduction to Slashing Strike as a defense-breaker. Bleeding wins the fight against a high-endurance opponent.

**Ending:** The chieftain nods. The greatsword is yours.
*50 XP earned. Slashing Strike unlocked.*

**World connection:** The orc camp remains at the western forest edge. If the quest was completed — orcs leave the player alone in an orc body. The veteran occasionally nods.

### Scout `scout`
**Weapon:** Short Bow | **Skill:** 🏹 Aimed Shot — ranged, 20% vulnerability

#### Quest: "The Road to Valdmar"

*[Text on possession]*
> *A light body. Quiet. At home in the forest.*
> *A merchant convoy sets out at dawn tomorrow.*
> *The road needs checking. Word is someone's been moving out there.*

**Objective:** Walk the road between Ashworth and Valdmar, mark dangerous spots with arrows — where someone camped, where tracks are suspicious, where ambush positions are favorable. Aimed Shot leaves a marker on a point — not damage, but a signal.

**Mechanic:** Introduction to ranged precision and reconnaissance use of the bow. Vulnerability as a "target marker" — the scout tags a threat for others.

**Ending:** Road checked. Three points marked. The convoy will have fair warning.
*50 XP earned. Aimed Shot unlocked.*

**World connection:** This is the exact road to Valdmar. In Chapter 2 the marked points will be exactly where things first went wrong.

### Shaman `shaman`
**Weapon:** Staff | **Skill:** 🐺 Summon Wolf — summons a wolf ally

#### Quest: "The Spirit's Call"

*[Text on possession]*
> *The world is full of voices. Trees speak. The wind carries names.*
> *Night. Need to reach the ancient stone and call out.*
> *Spirits only come to those who know how to listen.*

**Objective:** Reach the ancient stone on the clearing between zones at night. On the way — no fighting, only evasion. Spirits sense aggression and won't come. At the stone — the ritual: three circles, three staff strikes into the ground, the call.

**Mechanic:** Introduction to controlling the summoned wolf as a separate combat unit. The wolf ally responds to commands — attack, hold, retreat.

**Ending:** The spirit comes. A wolf steps from the shadows. Watches. Stays.
*50 XP earned. Summon Wolf unlocked.*

**World connection:** The stone on the clearing is the one marked on the Chapter 1 map near the Wind zone. If the ritual was performed, at night a wolf shadow is sometimes visible there. The Nameless Stranger notices.

### Spirit Wolf `spirit_wolf`
**Weapon:** Staff | **Skill:** 💚 Heal — HP restoration, 1.5 sec cast

#### Quest: "The Wounded"

*[Text on possession]*
> *The forest speaks of pain. Someone is lying nearby.*
> *Not a beast. A person. Breathing hard.*
> *The forest spirits do not leave the dying without reason.*

**Objective:** Find the injured traveler in the forest between the hills and the Misty Lake. Get there before the wolves (not malicious — just hungry). Heal them: Heal requires a 1.5-second cast — it cannot be interrupted. Protect while casting.

**Mechanic:** Introduction to Heal under pressure. Can't just stand still — maintain distance, heal, hold out.

**Ending:** The traveler opens their eyes. Looks at the spirit wolf strangely. Says: *"I saw something like this in Valdmar. Someone there... helped too. But didn't leave afterward."*
*100 XP earned. Heal unlocked.*

**World connection:** This traveler is the first person to mention Valdmar in connection with something resembling a Sphere. A hook for Chapter 2.

## Bandits

Bandit bodies are a special category. The Sphere finds itself inside people with specific orders from the boss. Complete the task, sabotage it, or simply leave — the player's choice. There are consequences either way.

---

### Bandit Archer `bandit_archer`
**Weapon:** Long Bow | **Skill:** 🏹 Long Shot — ranged, 20% CD reset

#### Quest: "Cover the Retreat"

*[Text on possession]*
> *Something went wrong. The camp is packing up.*
> *Three village guards are following the trail.*
> *The boss shouts: — Hold them off!*

**Objective:** Three waves of guards. Don't kill — deter. Arrows into the ground ahead of them, into shields, over their heads. Long Shot works at maximum range — they won't reach you if you hold position. CD reset keeps the pace up.

**Alternative:** Miss deliberately. Let the guards catch up. The camp gets taken, but no one gets hurt.

**Ending (completed):** Camp escaped. Guards came up empty. *50 XP. Long Shot unlocked.*
**Ending (sabotaged):** Camp captured. Bandits arrested. Village quieter. You leave the body.

**World connection:** If the camp escaped — it relocates into the hills. If captured — the hills are quieter. This affects how many bandits are in the mine dungeon.

---

### Crossbowman `bandit_crossbow`
**Weapon:** Crossbow | **Skill:** 🎯 Piercing Bolt — pierces 3 targets, 20% roots

#### Quest: "The Precise Shot"

*[Text on possession]*
> *The boss points at the gate of an old fort.*
> *A lock. Heavy. Key is lost.*
> *"You're the best shot. Find a way."*

**Objective:** A shot through three apertures in the lock mechanism — the bolt must thread through each one along a precise trajectory. Piercing Bolt passes through 3 targets — if the angle is right, all three locking pins are severed in one shot.

**Mechanic:** Introduction to piercing mechanics. Task: find the angle and distance. Several attempts.

**Ending:** The lock falls. Behind the gate — an old storeroom. Not empty.
*50 XP. Piercing Bolt unlocked.*

**World connection:** The storeroom holds supplies that went missing from Valdmar two months ago. The loot is not ordinary.

---

### Spearman `bandit_spear`
**Weapon:** Spear | **Skill:** 🗡 Lunge — melee, 30% knockback

#### Quest: "Night Watch"

*[Text on possession]*
> *Dark. Quiet. Your shift.*
> *Camp perimeter. Three hours to dawn.*
> *Something is moving in the bushes.*

**Objective:** Three threat waves in the night — not guards, not bandits. Something from the forest. Pebbles rolling in from the dark, a bear passing too close, shadows that shouldn't move. Lunge is the only way to push the threat back before it enters the camp.

**Mechanic:** Introduction to reactive Lunge use. The enemy appears suddenly — need distance and knockback before it deals damage.

**Ending:** Dawn. Camp intact. No one woke up. Only you know what happened in the night.
*50 XP. Lunge unlocked.*

**World connection:** The shadows that moved were not random. The first hint of something that will fully arrive in Chapter 2.

---

### Brute `bandit_brute`
**Weapon:** Hammer | **Skill:** 🔨 Crushing Strike — AoE r60, 20% armor crush

#### Quest: "Bring It Down"

*[Text on possession]*
> *The boss points at an old watchtower by the road.*
> *"Guards will use it. Take it down."*
> *Big body. Heavy hammer. Simple job.*

**Objective:** Four support columns. Each takes several hits. Crushing Strike — AoE — catches multiple columns at once if you position correctly. The tower starts leaning — get out before it collapses.

**Alternative:** Knock out only one column — the tower tilts but stands. Guards can use it, but barely. A half-measure of sabotage.

**Ending (full):** Tower down. Road open for the bandits.
**Ending (partial):** Tower standing. Barely.
*50 XP. Crushing Strike unlocked.*

**World connection:** The tower is marked on the Chapter 1 map. Its state is visible on the road to Valdmar in Chapter 2 — collapsed or leaning.

Quests are harder — enemies stronger, conditions tighter.

| Crossbow Master | 🎯 Snare Bolt (roots) | Hold a prisoner in place while they're being searched for |
