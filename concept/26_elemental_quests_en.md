# Elemental Quests — Chapter 1

Elementals are not people and not animals. They have no language, no plans, no morality.
Only the element. Only instinct.

An elemental's quest is experience of the world through its nature.
Fire reaches for warmth. Water fills the void. Earth holds its shape. Wind finds a way.

The Sphere inside an elemental doesn't think in words — it feels.

---

## Fire — Ash Grove

---

### Spark `spark`
**Skill:** 🔥 Spark — T1, ranged, base 6, cd 2 sec

#### Quest: "The Swarm"

*[Text on possession]*
> *Hot. Bright. Fast.*
> *Where are the others? There should be others here.*
> *The wind scattered everyone. Cold alone.*

**Objective:** Find five Sparks scattered across the grove and gather the swarm at the old vent. Each Spark glows dimmer — it has cooled in isolation. You must reach it first: if the cold takes hold, the Spark will go out. When the swarm gathers together — they flare up as one.

**Mechanic:** Training in speed and aerial navigation. First use of Spark — not as an attack, but as a beacon: a shot toward a cooling Spark warms it and draws it toward you.

**Ending:** The swarm at the vent. Heat. Unity. Everything is right.
*50 XP earned. Spark unlocked.*

**World connection:** After this quest, the swarm of Sparks stays together at the vent. The player passing by sees them — and they don't attack if they were among them.

### Asher `asher`
**Skill:** 🔥 Fire Arrow — T2, 5 projectiles, r150

#### Quest: "Markings"

*[Text on possession]*
> *Slow. Heavy. Long ago.*
> *The western slope — not our territory. But something went in there.*
> *The smell of water. The smell of something foreign.*
> *Need to leave markings so they don't return.*

**Objective:** Walk the boundary of the grove's western slope and leave burning spots at six points — these are territorial markings. Each spot must last 10 seconds before going out (Asher dying leaves a zone for 3 sec, but here it needs longer). Fire Arrow hits in a fan — one shot can place several markings if you position correctly.

**Mechanic:** Training in zone coverage through the projectile fan. Positioning so one shot covers the maximum number of points.

**Ending:** All six points burning. The smell of water is gone. The slope is ours.
*100 XP earned. Fire Arrow unlocked.*

**World connection:** The markings on the slope are visible for several minutes. If the player enters the fire zone through the western slope — there's less resistance from Splashers there (temporary buffer).

## Water — Misty Lake

---

### Splasher `splasher`
**Skill:** 🧊 Ice Shard — T1, ranged, base 7, cd 2 sec

#### Quest: "The Big Fish"

*[Text on possession]*
> *Water. Cold. Good.*
> *There, in the depths — a big fish. It's been slipping away for three days.*
> *Fast. Smart. But not smarter than water.*

**Objective:** Drive the large fish to the shallows. It retreats to the deep on direct approach — need to flank. Ice Shard slows on hit: a precise shot reduces the fish's speed and buys time to cut off its path to open water. Three hits — the fish is in the shallows.

**Mechanic:** Training in kiting and slowing. Ice Shard doesn't deal much damage — but the slow decides the outcome.

**Ending:** The fish in the shallows. Prey taken. The water is satisfied.
*50 XP earned. Ice Shard unlocked.*

**World connection:** That very "rare fish" that Innkeeper Paul wanted in the old test quest. Now it appears as an honest result of an elemental's quest.

### Fogger `fogger`
**Skill:** 🧊 Ice Arrow — T2, AoE r45

#### Quest: "The Fog is Leaving"

*[Text on possession]*
> *The fog is thinning. This is wrong.*
> *Sun breaking through. This should not be.*
> *The northern shore is almost visible straight through.*
> *Need to restore the cover.*

**Objective:** Move along the northern shore and thicken the fog at five points. Fogger exhales — that is the Ice Arrow: a cloud of cold that settles on the water and creates thick fog. AoE r45 — one use covers the area. Must precisely cover all five points before the sun scatters them again.

**Mechanic:** Training in AoE coverage and timing. The window between "sun scattered" and "sun scatters again" narrows at the third point.

**Ending:** Fog thick. Shore invisible again. Right.
*100 XP earned. Ice Arrow unlocked.*

**World connection:** After this quest the fog on the lake is denser than before. The player in main quest 2 goes to Aquaris through thicker fog — harder to navigate but Splashers don't aggro.

## Earth — Stone Hills

---

### Pebble `pebble`
**Skill:** 🪨 Pebble — T1, ranged, base 9, 20% armor crush

#### Quest: "A Place in the Wall"

*[Text on possession]*
> *Heavy. Stable. Right.*
> *There — a breach in the western wall of the cave.*
> *Without it the ceiling will start to settle.*
> *Need to take the place. Become part of it.*

**Objective:** Get through a network of tunnels to a specific point in the cave wall and stand there. The tunnels are narrow — Pebble is slow, some passages require stunning an obstacle (another stone or a Mudder blocks the way). Pebble throw — a precise toss that knocks a block out of the passage. When you arrive — you stand there. The wall holds.

**Mechanic:** Training in precise throwing. Pebble here is not a combat weapon but a clearing tool. 20% armor crush = destruction of obstacle.

**Ending:** The breach closed. The ceiling holds. Quiet. Good.
*50 XP earned. Pebble unlocked.*

**World connection:** The western wall holds. If the quest is not completed — in Quest 3 (mine dungeon) one of the tunnels is blocked by a collapse. If completed — the tunnel is open and there's a shortcut to Sven.

---

### Mudder `mudder`
**Skill:** 🪨 Stone Spike — T2, cross 4×260px, 20% armor pierce

#### Quest: "Roots Seek Water"

*[Text on possession]*
> *Dry. Long dry.*
> *Somewhere deeper there is water. You can feel it.*
> *Need to grow there. Through stone. Slowly.*

**Objective:** Grow roots through three layers of rock to the underground stream. Each layer is a separate obstacle: hard rock (needs Stone Spike — it hits in a cross, pierces in four directions at once), a competitor's roots (need to displace), and a narrow crack (need to direct the shoot precisely).

**Mechanic:** Training in the cross strike. Stone Spike is not point-targeted but directional in a fan: need to position so the cross covers all four points of the obstacle simultaneously.

**Ending:** Water found. Roots reached it. Slow. For a long time.
*100 XP earned. Stone Spike unlocked.*

**World connection:** The underground stream in the mine. If the quest is completed — there are no Mudders at the stream in the dungeon (their territory is watered). If not — three Mudders there instead of one.

## Wind — Wind Peaks

---

### Gusty `gusty`
**Skill:** 💨 Gust — T1, ranged, base 5, cd 2 sec

#### Quest: "The Race"

*[Text on possession]*
> *Fast. Even faster.*
> *Three others wait at the lower ravine.*
> *Whoever reaches the peak first — that's who it is.*

**Objective:** A race of three Gusties from the base to the top of the cliff. The straight path is slow: headwind pushes back. Need to read the currents — Gust at the right moment in the right direction accelerates, a headwind slows down. The other Gusties use the same tricks.

**Mechanic:** Training in directional Gust use. Here Gust is not an attack but a boost: pushing yourself in the right direction through the air.

**Ending:** The peak. The wind catches you. Three look up from below.
*50 XP earned. Gust unlocked.*

**World connection:** The race route is the same path as in main quest 4. If the race is completed — the Gusties on that path are not aggressive.

---

### Whistler `whistler`
**Skill:** 💨 Windcutter — T2, 3 whirlwinds cone 45°

#### Quest: "The False Note"

*[Text on possession]*
> *The wind should sing.*
> *But today — not like that. Something cuts the song in the middle.*
> *Somewhere on the southern slope. Need to find it.*

**Objective:** Find the source of the "false note" — something is disrupting the air currents on the southern slope. Whistler feels vibration: the closer the source, the clearer the direction. Windcutter — three whirlwinds in a cone — when fired toward the source creates a counter-current: if the vibration matched — the whirlwinds spin brighter. This is the navigation.

Source: a foreign metal spike sticks out of the rock — someone drove it in. It cuts the air wrong. Remove it.

**Ending:** The spike torn out. The wind sings evenly again.
On the spike — a marking. Not local. Not from hunters. Unknown.
*100 XP earned. Windcutter unlocked.*

**World connection:** The spike with the marking — a lore item. The Nameless Stranger, if shown it: stares for a long time, says nothing, walks away. The first physical trace of what is coming in Chapter 2.
