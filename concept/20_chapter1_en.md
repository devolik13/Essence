# 20. Chapter 1 — Awakening

---

## Atmosphere

Chapter 1 is a quiet immersion into a living fantasy world.
No horror of the Void, no global threat. This world has existed
by its own rules for thousands of years and has no idea
about the Steam World, Spheres, or the Void.

**The Sphere is invisible.** It inhabits the body of a creature —
and from the outside everything looks completely normal.
No one sees a foreign consciousness. People see their neighbor,
the familiar hunter, the local farmer.

**Memory is fuzzy.** The Sphere receives the body's muscle memory,
basic skills, and vague outlines of relationships — faces feel familiar
but the details of conversations are blurred. Like a dream almost recalled.
This can be used naturally:
*"I forgot — which way to the lake again?"*
People don't suspect. People help.

**Every body is limited.** A warrior can't read an encrypted diary.
A wolf can't hold a sword. To go further — you need to switch bodies.
This isn't a weakness. It's a way to see the world through different eyes.

**Body Quest mechanic.** When the Sphere inhabits a new body —
it may have unfinished business. These appear as optional side quests.
Whether to complete them is the player's choice. But each one reveals
a piece of this world from the inside.

## Starter Bodies at the Resurrection Stone

Three local adventurers stand near the stone — they gathered here
on purpose, drawn by the legend of the Elemental Guardians.
They haven't dared to try yet. The Sphere inhabits one of them —
the player chooses.

---

### Conrad — Warrior

Former guard of a merchant caravan. Solid, quiet, reliable.
Settled in Ashworth three months ago — says "for the winter",
but winter has passed. Likes the tavern, drinks moderately, pays honestly.

**Bert knows him** — they've hunted together. He trusts him.
**Aldric respects him** — Conrad promised to bring a stone from the grove
and never did. Aldric is waiting. Silently.

*Conrad's body quest:* An old receipt in his pocket.
Someone owes him money at a nearby farmstead. Or he owes someone.
Memory is unclear.

**Playstyle:** melee, high armor, slow but powerful.

---

### Eyla — Mage

Daughter of a traveling merchant. Her father left six months ago,
she stayed — says "it's interesting here", meaning the old ruins
beyond the hills. Reads everything she can find.
Absent-minded in daily life, sharp on details.

**Mira is her friend** — they often sit together at the smithy in the evenings.
**Innkeeper Paul** remembers that she borrowed something last week
and hasn't paid it back. A small thing. But Paul remembers everything.

*Eyla's body quest:* An unfinished letter to her father on the desk.
Three weeks unsent. And an unfamiliar scroll with symbols
she had almost worked out.

**Playstyle:** magic, ranged, physically weak —
but can read diaries, scrolls, and encrypted texts.

---

### Ren — Archer

Hunter. Quiet, observant, knows every trail in the area.
Sometimes helps Bert. Says little — but when she speaks, it's precise.

**Bert trusts her** as a partner.
**Mira** is her old acquaintance — sometimes Ren brings her herbs from the forest.
**Farmer Ed** knows her — sees her sometimes near the herd.

*Ren's body quest:* Three days ago she promised Bert
to check the traps at the northern stream. She didn't.
Bert won't remind her — but the traps are waiting.

**Playstyle:** ranged, fast, knows forest paths —
some routes to the Guardian zones open only through her memory.

## Body Quest Mechanic

When the Sphere inhabits any creature in the world —
it may have unfinished business. The game shows these as optional quests.

The quest lives within the logic of the creature:

| Body | Example Quest | Essence |
|---|---|---|
| Rabbit | Gather carrots from the garden | Instinct. The field is close. Carrots smell good. |
| Wolf | Chase a deer at the eastern stream | The pack is hungry. The trail is fresh. |
| Orc | Attack Farmer Ed's homestead | The warchief ordered it. The orcs are waiting outside. |
| Farmer | Protect the herd from wolves tonight | The cows are restless. A calf was taken yesterday. |
| Guard | Check the suspicious noise at the gate | Duty. Post. Night shift. |
| Merchant | Deliver a package to the next village | A contract. The money was already taken upfront. |

**Moral choice** is built into certain quests.
The orc is supposed to attack the homestead — but the Sphere decides how:
carry out the attack, sabotage it from within, or simply
leave the body and let the orc stand confused at the gate.

The game doesn't judge. The game shows consequences.

## Location — Village of Ashworth and Surroundings

**Ashworth** — a small village at the center of the map.
Farmers, hunters, a blacksmith, an innkeeper.
Peaceful life by its own rules.

Four natural zones surround the village:

| Zone | Element | Location |
|---|---|---|
| Ash Grove | Fire | Burned forest to the east, hot springs |
| Misty Lake | Water | Lake and river to the north, eternal fog |
| Stone Hills | Earth | Rocky cliffs and caves to the west |
| Wind Peaks | Wind | High cliffs to the south, constant wind |
